How Much Does A Website Cost?

Learn more about what the average cost of a website is in South Africa, in 2023. Remember, each company charges different amounts for their website design services – and this cost usually depends on the amount of work and effort the service will require.

It is essential to be mindful that many companies use the fact that business owners do not have a lot of website design and development knowledge to take advantage of them – by overcharging them. This does not mean that every expensive website is overpriced – prices vary, so let’s discuss it. In this article, we break down the various costs that can occur when having a website designed and developed.

So, how much does a website cost in South Africa, let’s explore posibilites.

Here’s a list of services you could potentially pay for when having your website professionally designed:

The below design-related services are not offered by all design companies, and some companies may have completely different factors that impact their pricing. These are just examples of what your money could potentially be going towards.

1. Branding

If your business is new, or you are doing a rebrand, the cost of branding may be added to the cost of your website design. Branding usually includes a logo design and corporate identity. At Buys Management, we offer a full branding package that includes: logo design, business card design, profile design, email signature design, and 10 social media image templates – this is not included in our website design fee, but can be added.

Typically a price for a logo design in South Africa can range from R400 – 1500.

2. UX Design (User Experience Design)

Before your website gets designed, a process called User Experience Design is typically completed, usually by a UX Designer. This process includes UX research (general research, user interviews, user surveys, creating user personas, etc.), Wireframing, Prototyping, and Testing. UX design focuses on the structure of your website and how users interact with it.

A UX designer can cost anywhere from R110 an hour to R280 an hour.

3. UI Design (User Interface Design)

User interface design focuses on the aesthetic elements of your website, which users will interact with, such as your menu bars, buttons, icons, typography, colours, and more. This is where you really start to see your website come to life visually.

A UI designer can cost anywhere from R110 an hour to R300 an hour.

Here’s a list of services you could potentially pay for when having your website professionally developed:

The below list of website development-related services are not offered by all companies, and some companies may have other factors that influence their pricing. These are examples of costs that can be encured when having a website professionally developed.

1. Domain registration and renewals

Domain registration is very quick and easy and this is something you can even do yourself. Depending the domain you want, it can cost roughly R80-R200 a year. Sometimes a domain can cost thousands of rands if you are buying it from a reseller or the domain you want is being used by someone else and you convince them to sell it to you.

It is important to pick the right domain because once you purchase and register a domain, you cannot edit it. For example, if your business is called “Example” and the domain you want is “example.com” you need to make sure that the spelling is 100% correct before checking out.

2. Monthly Hosting

Website hosting is a fee that you have to pay every month, typically on the 1st. This is the monthly expense you pay, to keep your website online. Website hosting cost usually start at R45 per month and can get very expensive the bigger your website becomes.

3. Themes

There are many themes out there, some are free and you pay for – when it comes to themes you usually get your monies worth. Paid themes offer support, are frequently updated, etc. While free themes have more bugs and are not updated frequently. There are many marketplaces where you can shop for free and paid themes, such as ThemeForest.

4. Plugins

Plugins give your website functionality without the need to code it from scratch. For example, instead of coding online store functionality such as “add to cart” and “checkout” features, we can simply install a plugin such as WooCommerce. Almost all plugins have free versions that gives you basic functions, and premium versions, which allow you to truly customise your website and create a unique experience for your website visitors. Plugin costs vary, and are usually paid monthly.

5. Development

You have options when it comes to how your website will be developed. There are drag and drop tools such as Elementor and Divi, or you can have your website coded by a developer. Some drag-and-drop tools offer free versions, while most come with a monthly, or annual fee. At Buys Management, we use a combination of these tools and custom code to speed up our development process.

A mid-level developer that can realistically code your website design in South Africa can cost on average R285 per hour.

Here’s a list of services you could potentially pay for when having your website maintenance done professionally:

1. Website maintenance

Website maintenance is the tasks associated with keeping your website performing optimally. All websites need to be maintained, or risk serious issues such as being vulnerable to cyber attacks and your website crashing. We have an informative blog post on what website maintenance is, why its important and list the maintenance tasks you should be doing weekly, monthly and yearly that you can read if you’d like to learn more about this topic.

2. SEO (Search Engine Optimisation)

An SEO strategy is key to having a successful website. SEO brings organic (free) traffic to your website from search results on search engines such as Google. SEO aims to improve your website’s position on search results pages, and usually the higher your ranking, the more people will see it.

If you are not sure if SEO would benefit your business, think about this – when you are searching for something to purchase online, do you shop on the first page of Google, or go all the way back? You’ll probably find what you want and need on page 1. That is what SEO is all about. If you can get your business on page 1 when your potential customers are ready to buy, you’ll get that free traffic to your website – and the sales that come with it.

3. Additional features and integrations

You’ll very quickly realise that you want to make some changes to your website – either to increase conversion, meet new customer needs or to simply improve your websites user experience. Adding additional features and integrations isn’t usually part of your website maintenance, and as a result, can come with its own costs.
