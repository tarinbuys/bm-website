Introduction

Dazo is a note-taking web-based platform that offers organizational tools including to-do lists, bookmarking, project tracking and more. Dazo wants to be the go-to productivity platform for professionals.

Overview

The Product:

A landing page for a productivity application aimed at professionals who prefer using digital tools instead of traditional pen and paper

The Problem:

Creating a landing page that encourages the users to purchase a monthly plan or to use the free trail of the application, in a simple yet aesthetically pleasing manner.

Goal Statement:

Our landing page will show users how to use the Dazo application which will effect users who prefer digital tools to stay organised and productive. We will measure the effectiveness of the landing page by tracking the number of users who purchased a package or started the free trail.

User Research

1

Not Easy To Use

2

Restricted Space

On mobile, the interfaces can get very cluttered and restrict what the user can do.

3

Providing a free trial and keeping the monthly fee low overcomes this pain point.

Takeaways

1. User Research.
Due to this project being for a fictional company, the user research was not done in collaboration with actual users. UX research such as surveys and user interviews would have been greatly beneficial to this project.

2. Team Work
I enjoy the ideas, feedback and joint knowledge that comes with collaborating with a team. For this project, I worked alone. I tried my best to be as open minded as possible but I do think not being able to bounce ideas around would have added more value to this project.
